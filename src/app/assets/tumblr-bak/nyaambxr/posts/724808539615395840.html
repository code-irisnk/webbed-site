facts-i-just-made-up:

There is, in fact, at least one species of Gramineae to reflect each and every single wavelength of light distinguishable to the human eye. Thus, grass is every color. Grass was first classified by Roy Gerardi Biv himself though, who was of course colorblind. Hence his name for it, the “Grey-Ass” plant, or “Grass.”
